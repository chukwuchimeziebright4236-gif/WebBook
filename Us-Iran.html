Mixed Signals Over US-Iran Peace Talks in Doha

There are conflicting reports coming out of the Middle East and Washington regarding sudden, unexpected diplomacy. US President Donald Trump announced on Truth Social that a highly anticipated meeting between the US and Iran was scheduled for today in Doha, Qatar.

However, Iran’s Deputy Foreign Minister partially downplayed this statement, clarifying to reporters that no "technical talks" were officially on the schedule for this week, even though indirect consultations through international mediators remain ongoing.

Meanwhile, international energy markets are responding to the geopolitical uncertainty. Brent crude oil prices climbed back above $72 per barrel as traders react to the diplomatic ambiguity and recent tit-for-tat military tensions around the vital Strait of Hormuz shipping lane.
