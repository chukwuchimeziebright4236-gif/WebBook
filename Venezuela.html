Venezuela Earthquake Death Toll Rises Past 1,700

The humanitarian crisis in Venezuela has deepened significantly following last week's devastating back-to-back earthquakes. Disaster response officials confirmed this morning that the official death toll has tragically climbed to 1,719, with more than 5,000 people treated for injuries.

Emergency management agencies report that over 15,000 families have been heavily displaced or affected. Initial structural damage assessments indicate that upwards of 58,000 buildings have been severely compromised, partially collapsed, or completely destroyed across the impacted regions.

Rescue teams continue to clear debris as international aid begins to trickle into the hardest-hit towns. Local authorities are pleading for medical supplies, clean drinking water, and temporary shelter equipment.
